## ✦ BRAND ANALYSIS — Deconstruct

---

**The Brand**
Deconstruct was launched by Malini Adapureddy in 2020 with one very clear mission — make science-backed skincare actually accessible to real people. Not intimidating, not overpriced, not full of claims nobody can verify. Just honest, effective products built for Indian skin. In 2024 alone the brand grew 10x from January to December and achieved profitability — rare for a D2C brand at this stage. They're now eyeing ₹500 crore. For a brand that started by simply telling people what's actually in their products, that's a remarkable place to be.

---

**What They're Getting Right**

The thing that genuinely impresses me about Deconstruct is that they chose education over hype at a time when every other skincare brand was doing the opposite. While competitors were throwing celebrities at problems and making miracle claims, Deconstruct was quietly explaining what Niacinamide actually does and why you shouldn't layer certain actives together. That decision built something no marketing budget can manufacture — actual trust.

Their content doesn't talk down to their audience and it doesn't overcomplicate things either. It sits right in that sweet spot where a complete beginner feels confident enough to start and an ingredient-obsessed skincare nerd still finds it worth watching. That's genuinely hard to do. Their influencer strategy reflects this too — they brief creators on technical content for bottom-funnel audiences while using personality-led creators for awareness, which led to campaigns crossing 15 million views with strong engagement. It's not just pretty content. It's content that actually moves people through a purchase decision.

Their early entry into quick commerce — Swiggy Instamart, Zepto, Blinkit — has contributed 10-15% of revenues with 200% month-on-month growth in some channels. For a skincare brand, that's significant. It means when someone runs out of their serum on a Wednesday night, Deconstruct is the brand that shows up. That's not just distribution strategy. That's becoming a habit.

---

**What's Unique**

Most skincare brands sell you a transformation. Deconstruct sells you understanding. And that's a fundamentally different relationship with a customer. When you understand *why* a product works, you don't just repurchase — you become an advocate because you can actually explain it to someone else. That word-of-mouth flywheel is exactly why Deconstruct has grown the way it has without spending like a legacy brand. In a market where trends often overpower trust, they chose to build trust first — and the growth followed.

---

A Campaign Idea — "Unke Liye Bhi"

Deconstruct' s Gen Z audience knows their ingredients, follows their routines, and genuinely cares about their skin. But there's someone at home who doesn't and probably never will unless their kid shows them.

Indian parents grew up in a time when skincare meant soap and using any cream that they get at there nearest grocery store and sometimes not even that. They have real skin concerns like uneven tone, pigmentation, sun damage, dullness but nobody has ever spoken to them about how to treat. They're not going to find Deconstruct on their own because they are not willing to put in efforts for themselves. But their kids can bring it to them.

The campaign puts out content specifically designed to be shared with a parent, not about young skin but about theirs. The kind of thing a 22-year-old would genuinely forward to their family telling them that "this is actually useful."

For execution we can stays true to how Deconstruct already communicates, short educational reels and simple carousels, but written for a parent, not just a Gen Z audience. The Gen Z kid is the bridge, they already trust the brand, now they have a reason to pass that trust home. Content is designed to be saved and forwarded on WhatsApp, which is where Indian parents actually spend their time.
---

**The Bigger Picture**

Deconstruct figured out something most brands spend years trying to manufacture — that if you genuinely help people understand something, they come back. Every piece of content they make is essentially a small act of generosity, and that's built a community that doesn't just buy their products but believes in the brand. In a market full of noise, that kind of quiet confidence is the hardest thing to compete with.
